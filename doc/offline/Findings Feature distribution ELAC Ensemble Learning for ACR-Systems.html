Feature distribution

Posted on 31. Juli 2010 [Edit] Leave a comment (0)

Feature distribution

We investigated the feature distribution for Coreference Pairs across all used corpora to ensure a preferably balanced feature distribution for training and test corpora.

One interesting aspect is the difference in feature distribution between the original semeval data (used for training and performance measurement) mainly consisting of news texts and the domain text used (Tiger of San Pedro).

We restricted the documentation to a few interesting cases, providing the document containing all the exact data as an attachment.

PosTags (anaphora)

Distribution of PoS tags (anaphora) for coreference pairs

Overall tendencies:

  • The domain text contains substantially more pronouns as anaphora (both personal and possessive).
  • The number of proper nouns as anaphoric elements in news texts excels the number of proper nouns in the domain text. This can be explained with the elaborate description of single characters in a novel.
  • Note that dt usually goes together with nns or nn to form an NP, which shows that the domain text consists of less NPs (excluding proper nouns, as explained above) as anaphora than the standard semeval corpus.

Named entity type

Distribution of NETypes of coreference pairs

  • Obviously news articles deal with (comercial) organizations more often than they occur in a novel…
  • … whereas the novel deals more with the description and interaction of persons.
  • Note that there are less coreference/anaphora mentions labeled as named entities in the domain text in general.

Sentence Offset

Average sentence offset between anaphora and coreferent

The feature sentenceOffset determines the distance between anaphora and antecedent in sentences.

As the domain text consists of one long text (in contrast to many independent small texts in semeval_train50), the offset in sentences is substantially higher for the domain text.

Competing pronouns in the same sentence

Average number of competing pronouns in a sentence.

The number of (competing) pronouns in the same sentence is slightly higher in the domain text. As competing pronouns drastically reduce the probability of right guesses for ACR systems, this factor could be interesting for performance results.

Summary

The difference between the feature distribution of the semeval and domain texts can be easily explained considering the nature of both corpora.
The much higher distance between coreference pairs results from a relatively small list of characters, that reappear throughout one novel compared to several short news texts dealing with their own person or group of persons each. The same phenomenon accounts for the higher sentenceOffset.
The slightly higher frequence of pronouns found in the domain corpus can be explained with the elaborate description of single characters in a novel.
Obviously news articles deal with (comercial) organizations more often than they occur in a novel.
The distribution of part of speech tags in percent further strengthens the above assumption of pronoun usage in the domain corpus. It also shows that while news texts more often deal with proper names (nnp), the novel makes far more use of general nouns (nn/nns) which form noun phrases when combined with determiners (dt).
