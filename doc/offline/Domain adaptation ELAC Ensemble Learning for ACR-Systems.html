Domain Adaptation

General

After establishing a working ML infrastructure for anaphora and coreference resolution on news texts we decided to also use it on a different domain. We chose a Sherlock Holmes novel from the Tiger/Salsa corpus (provided by the Saarland University) to evaluate and compare the results.
As this corpus follows another annotation style we first had to convert it into MMAX2 format. The routine to do so is included in ELAC.
Note that the following results are based on the single text mentioned above, which is considerabely shorter (7600 words approx.) than the corpora we used for general training/testing. However, results are still indicating the main differences.

Feature distribution

For a detailed comparison of feature distributions in the semeval corpus and the domain text please refer to the article Feature distribution.

Machine Learning results

To make domain adaptation comparable on a simple scale, we chose the best ACR system (BART) as a baseline against which the machine learning results are compared, as we did in our original ML approach.

Overall results (F-Score)

Comparing ML, baseline and average results for domain adaptation (F-Score)

Although results for domain adaptation are considerably worse than the original ML results, both share the ML classified output located between average results and the baseline.

Recall

Comparing ML, baseline and average results for domain adaptation (Recall)

Recall results for domain adaptation are devastating, most time not even surpassing the average result of all three ACR systems. This most probably stems from the classifiers being provided with too much wrong data, eventually believing it to be true.

Precision

Comparing ML, baseline and average results for domain adaptation (Precision)

Precision results for domain adaptation are rather good, with all classifiers providing better than average results.

Comment

Due to the differences between the originally used news texts and the novel text which are very well reflected in the feature distribution, results for domain adaptation poor, as expected. While classifiers in most cases could not even lift the recall values above the average, precision values were acceptable, but still lacking the boost they provided in the original ML process.
But considering all the differences between the corpora mentioned above, values close to 40% still seem to be an acceptable result for our domain adaptation approach.
